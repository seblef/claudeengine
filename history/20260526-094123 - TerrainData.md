## Design decisions

### Heightmap encoding
`uint16_t` samples map linearly from `[0, 65535]` to `[min_height_, max_height_]`
in metres. This is the canonical GPU-friendly 16-bit format (R16_UNORM);
`GetRawData()` returns the buffer directly so no conversion is needed for GPU
upload.

### Bilinear interpolation (`GetHeight`)
World-space (x, z) is divided by `meters_per_texel_` to obtain fractional texel
coordinates, then the four surrounding texels are bilinearly blended. Out-of-
range coordinates are clamped at `HeightAt`, matching typical terrain engine
behaviour.

### Normal computation (`GetNormal`)
Central differences over one texel step in each axis give tangent vectors
along X and Z. The cross product `tangent_x √ó tangent_z` yields an upward-
pointing normal (Y-up, right-hand convention) which is then normalised.
The two-texel span used by central differences means the normal is a
smooth approximation rather than a per-triangle normal, which is appropriate
for rendering and physics queries.

### No platform headers
`TerrainData` only includes `<cstdint>`, `<vector>`, `<algorithm>`, `<cmath>`,
`<cassert>`, and `core/Vec3f.h`. No OpenGL or platform headers are present,
in compliance with the issue requirement.

## Output to keep in mind

- `TerrainData` is intentionally minimal ‚Äî LOD, streaming, and normal maps are
  out of scope for this issue.
- The `abstract` dependency in `terrain/CMakeLists.txt` is declared now to
  signal the intended direction (GPU texture wrapper will live here later) but
  no abstract headers are currently included.
- Future terrain classes (patches, renderer integration) should link `terrain`
  and NOT reach into `gldevices` directly.
